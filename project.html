Project 1: Gesture Controlled Audio System

Users can interact with the system by bending the flex sensors to trigger specific audio responses. Each bending gesture corresponds to a unique audio file, enabling users to control the audio playback and generate different sounds based on their gestures.Users can also interact with the system through voice commands by producing loud sounds detected by the microphone. The system responds to loud sounds by playing predefined audio files, enhancing user engagement and interaction.The system provides auditory feedback through the speaker, confirming the successful detection and execution of user gestures and voice commands. The audible responses help users understand and navigate the system, enhancing the overall userÂ experience.

SOFTWARE IMPLEMENTATION

Initialization:

Upon powering on, the Arduino initializes the flex sensors, microphone,MP3 module, and audio amplifier. The system performs a startup routine to ensure all components are functioning correctly. Sensor Input Detection: The Arduino continuously reads the analog values from the flex sensors and microphone. Based on the sensor readings, the Arduino identifies the type of gesture performed by the user (bending a flex sensor) or detects a loud sound from the microphone. Gesture Recognition: When a flex sensor detects a bending gesture (e.g., bending a specific finger), the Arduino triggers a corresponding audio file to play through the speaker via the MP3 module. Each flex sensor is associated with a unique audio file, allowing for distinct sound responses to different bending gestures. Voice Command Recognition: If the microphone detects a sound above the predefined threshold, the Arduino activates a specific audio file to play through the speaker, indicating a response to the detected loud sound. Audio Playback Control: The Arduino controls the playback of audio files on the MP3 module by sending serial commands to select and play the desired audio files. The audio amplifier amplifies the audio signal from the MP3 module, ensuring clear and audible sound output through the speaker. Volume Control: he system includes volume control functionality, allowing users to adjust the volume level of the audio output using predefined gestures or voice commands. The Arduino modifies the volume settings on the MP3

HARDWARE IMPLEMENTATION

Hardware Components Flex Sensors: Ten flex sensors are connected to the analog input pins (A0 to A9) of the Arduino microcontroller. These sensors change their resistance based on the degree of bend, allowing them to detect and measure various bending gestures. Arduino Microcontroller: The Arduino serves as the central processing unit of the system, receiving input signals from the flex sensors and microphone, and controlling the audio playback through the MP3 module and audio amplifier. MP3-TF-16P MP3 SD Card Module: This module stores pre-recorded audio files corresponding to different gestures and loud sounds. The Arduino communicates with the MP3 module via a serial connection to trigger the playback of specific audio files based on the sensor inputs and microphone readings. PAM84403 Audio Amplifier: The audio amplifier is connected to the MP3 module and speaker to boost the audio signal, ensuring clear and audible sound output. Electret Microphone (MIC): The microphone detects loud sounds and sends an analog signal to the Arduino. When a sound above a predefined threshold is detected, the Arduino triggers a specific audio file to play through the speaker.
